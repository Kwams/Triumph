About Us

Triumph in Life Inc.

Triumph in Life, Inc. was founded in 2008 by a then-public school teacher who saw the need for mentoring and other enrichment activities for her “high-functioning” students who had severe academic deficits students.

It was her vision to create a continuum of academic, mentoring, and awareness programs that would meet the needs of her students by filling in the gaps left by traditional schools that have not lived up to the promise of No Child Left Behind.

After serving deserving students with high-quality mentoring and educating parents, teachers, and school leaders on best practices for educating students with specific learning disabilities, in 2014 Triumph in Life, Inc. started Triumph Transitions Institute, now known as Triumph School.

As a result of the pandemic, and the realization of the impact on all students, in 2023 Triumph in Life, Inc. is launching Triumph on Wheels, an innovative way to bring educational services to students via the use of a bus to remediate academic deficits and improve reading and math skills for students in grades 4t - 8th in South Cobb and South Fulton. This program is open to all students and continues the continuum of services to students meeting them where they are.

Our Mission

Triumph is an accredited academic program that remediates academic weaknesses and provides a path to high school graduation and vocational training for students who need an alternative to traditional schools and other educational programs.

Our Founder

Cindy Lumpkin

Cindy Lumpkin is the Founder of Triumph in Life, Inc and serves as Director of Triumph School. She has been an educator for over 20 years and has a passion for teaching students who benefit from specialized instruction, particularly those in middle and high school who are behind.

Throughout her work as an educator, she has developed instructional programming that not only is successful in remediating academic deficits but developing self-advocacy skills and emotional intelligence in students. She provides instructional coaching and professional development for all levels of principals and their teachers and staff both in the public and private sectors. She has served as an inclusion and self-contained teacher for both middle and high school.
